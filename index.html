Introduction

Introduction

How does Reyna Grande grow from a girl who cannot fully use English into a writer with her own voice? How does an AmLa 80 student grow from not knowing MLA format to understanding academic writing better? How can immigration look different after learning about family pressure and sacrifice? What advice can make AmLa 80 easier and more useful for the next students? These questions are the main ideas of this webpage. This project shows that growth can happen through Reyna Grande’s writing journey, through learning academic writing in AmLa 80, through a deeper view of immigration, and through advice that can help the class become better for future students.

Book Evidence

Reyna Grande’s Growth as a Writer

Reyna Grande’s growth started with writing and language. In The Distance Between Us, she writes, “I wrote that even though I was now living far away from Mami and my country, I hadn't forgotten where I came from” (203). This shows that writing helped her remember Mami, Mexico, and her identity.

However, Reyna still struggled with English. When her teacher rejected her book, Reyna says, “I wanted to tell her she'd made a mistake and she must look at it again, but I didn't have the English words to say what I thought how I felt-so I said nothing at all” (204). She had ideas, but she could not fully speak for herself.

Reyna did not stop there. She promised herself, “One day, I promised myself, thinking about Mr. López's words, I will write a book that won't be rejected, one that will make my father proud” (206). Later, reading helped her English become stronger, and she was able to enter regular English classes (234). This shows her growth from being silent to slowly finding her voice as a writer.

Personal Growth

My Growth from AmLa 80 to AmLa 90

My biggest growth in AmLa 80 is learning academic writing. Before this class, I did not know what MLA format was. In China, my English writing practice was more like CET-4 writing. I usually only needed to answer the prompt. I did not really need to use quotes, page numbers, topic sentences, or quote sandwich. In AmLa 80, I learned that academic writing in the U.S. needs clearer structure. Quote sandwich helped me most because it taught me how to introduce evidence and explain it.

Low-stakes writing was also useful, even though it was hard for me. I write English slowly, so short writing time forced me to organize ideas faster. Google Slides helped me review MLA format and sentence rules. I also became a little more willing to use English in class. My speaking is still weak, but conferences, the Cajita Project, and workshops made me less afraid to express myself.

Text to Self

My View of Immigrants

Before AmLa 80, I mostly thought about immigration in a practical way. I thought many people immigrated because they wanted better income, better schools, and a better life. This is still true to me. When I was in China, my internship income was only about 3,000 RMB a month, and it was hard to save money after basic costs. So I understand why people want to come to a country with more opportunities.

However, this class helped me see that immigration is not only about opportunity. It also has pressure and sacrifice. As a new immigrant, I have to think about English, school, work, rent, and the future at the same time. My Cajita Project helped me show this idea. The small dollar bill represented opportunity, but also money pressure. The pen represented education because I hope school can give me a more stable future. The tea bag represented culture and memory because immigrants may leave their familiar life behind.

Now I think the American Dream is more complicated than I thought before. The article “Children of Poor Immigrants Rise, Regardless of Where They Come From” also made me think about the next generation. Maybe my life can become better here, but I do not think the dream will be fully finished in my generation. Maybe my hard work can create a better condition for the next generation. This is why my view changed: immigration is not just moving to a richer country. It is also a long process of sacrifice, pressure, and hope.

Advice

Advice for the AmLa 80 Teacher

1. Give clearer directions for group poster activities.

I think poster activities are useful. They can help students talk with classmates and practice English. But sometimes they take too much time. In my experience, it was also hard to divide the work in the group. Some students are not good at drawing, so they may feel more pressure. If the teacher gives clearer steps or a shorter time limit, this activity can be easier for students.

2. Add more small speaking practice.

I think speaking practice is helpful in AmLa 80. Some students, like me, can write slowly, but speaking is much harder. When I speak face to face, I cannot think about grammar and word choice very well. If the class has more small speaking activities, students can use the ideas from reading and writing. It can help writing and speaking together.

3. Practice quote sandwich more before papers.

Quote sandwich helped me a lot, but I think it needs more practice. It is not easy to introduce a quote and explain it clearly. If students can practice it a few more times before the paper, they may feel less confused. This can also make their evidence stronger.

Advice for Future AmLa 80 Students

1. Do not wait until the last minute.

Reading journals and papers take a lot of time. Reading the book is not fast, and writing responses also takes time. Papers need revision, grammar checking, and better word choices. If you wait until the weekend, you may feel very stressed. It is better to finish work earlier.

2. Make more appointments with the professor and tutors.

Appointments are useful, not only for DLA or required work. Talking with the professor or tutor helped me understand writing better. For example, I learned more about thesis statements and how to connect one main idea to body paragraphs. It also helped me practice speaking English, so I became a little less nervous.

3. Read The Distance Between Us carefully.

This book is used many times in AmLa 80. Later assignments often need examples or quotes from the book. If you read carefully and take some notes, the homework will be easier. Do not only read for finishing the chapter. Try to remember important scenes.

Conclusion

Conclusion

Growth is the main idea of this project. Reyna’s growth shows how a quiet immigrant student can slowly find a voice through writing. AmLa 80 also shows growth from not knowing MLA format to understanding academic writing better. The immigrant lessons changed the understanding of opportunity and sacrifice. The advice part shows that growth can also happen when a class becomes clearer and more useful. For the future, this growth should not stop after AmLa 80. In AmLa 90, there will still be harder readings and longer essays, but the next growth may be writing faster and speaking more naturally. As a new immigrant student, the future may not become easy so quickly. However, every class and every paper may become a small step. Maybe one day this student can transfer to a university, become a nurse, or continue the path of computer science and programming. The future is still not very clear, but it is more possible than before. Maybe the American Dream will take a long time, but this kind of growth can make the road more possible to walk.
